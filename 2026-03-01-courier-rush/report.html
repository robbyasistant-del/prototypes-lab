Courier Rush — Prototype Report

A compact management game designed for mobile: triage (accept/reject), dispatch (assign scarce couriers), and invest (upgrades) — all under a 2‑minute clock.

Prototype date: 2026-03-01 Genre: Management / Time pressure / Triage Play Catalog

1) Product Strategy

Target player

  • Players who like Overcooked‑style time pressure, but want a one‑handable, readable UI.
  • “Brain‑busy” players who enjoy prioritization, not twitch controls.

Positioning

  • 2‑minute shift sessions: clear timebox supports repeated plays.
  • Reputation acts as a soft health bar and a multiplier → decisions have meaning.
  • Orders are intentionally not all takeable → the skill is saying “no”.

Why it wins (unique value)

  • Readable triage UI: payout, deadline, and risk in one card.
  • Compact economy: cash for upgrades; rep for multiplier and end bonus.
  • Strong social hook potential: share a “dispatch score” for a daily/weekly ladder.

Audience expansion (future)

  • Cosmetic city districts, courier skins, and “company name” identity.
  • Daily modifiers (rain/traffic), VIP events, multi-stop routing (midcore).

2) Core Loop & Systems

Loop: Orders arrive → accept/reject → assign to courier → deliver on time for cash+rep → buy speed upgrades → deliver more reliably → end-of-shift bonus based on reputation.

Rewards

  • Immediate: delivery toast feedback, clear “late vs on-time” consequence.
  • Session: visible throughput (delivered count) + compounding upgrade power.
  • Meta (planned): company progression tree, city districts unlocking, courier collection.

Design levers

  • Queue cap and overflow penalties tune stress level.
  • VIP frequency tunes risk: higher payout + tight deadlines.
  • Upgrade curve (cost vs speed) tunes “snowball” vs “tightrope”.

3) SEO / ASO / SEM Plan

ASO

  • Keywords: delivery game, dispatch, management, time pressure, tycoon
  • Tagline: “Can you run the city’s fastest 2‑minute dispatch shift?”
  • Screens: 1) incoming order cards, 2) courier progress bars, 3) upgrade choice, 4) end score.

SEO

  • Web landing demo + “2-minute dispatch challenge” copy.
  • Content: “How triage mechanics create fun stress” + “why saying no is satisfying”.

SEM

  • Short ads: show VIP order, risky accept, late penalty, then upgrade to save next order.
  • Creative angle: “Your queue is full. What do you reject?”

4) KPIs

D1 retention
Target 25–35% (short sessions; meta progression will matter).
Runs per day
Target 2–5 (2-minute shift invites repeats).
Completion rate
Target 80%+ finish a shift (timebox ends regardless, reduces rage quit).
Upgrade purchase rate
Target 65%+ buy at least one upgrade in first 3 runs (clarity of economy).
Rep distribution
Median rep at end 55–80; too low means unfair, too high means no tension.
ARPU/LTV proxy
Rewarded ads after shift + cosmetic pass; track opt-in rate.

Instrumentation

  • Events: shift_start, order_spawn, order_assign(courier), order_reject, order_expire, delivery(late/on-time), upgrade_buy.
  • Key funnel: start → first assign → first delivery → first upgrade → finish shift → replay.

5) Experiments

Gameplay

  • Carry capacity: allow van to carry 2 orders (route batching) vs 1; measure depth vs complexity.
  • Decision prompt: “auto-reject overflow” vs “pause and decide” when queue full.
  • Skill expression: add limited “priority token” to protect a VIP from late penalty.

Meta

  • District unlock: each district adds new order types (fragile, refrigerated, bulky).
  • Daily dispatch: same order schedule for all players; global leaderboard.

6) Risks & Mitigations

  • Risk: Feels random/unfair (order spam).
    Mitigation: seed the order schedule; guarantee tutorial ramp; show predicted travel time and deadline clarity.
  • Risk: UI cognitive overload on small screens.
    Mitigation: progressive disclosure; simplify order cards; add “recommended courier” highlight.
  • Risk: Low long-term retention without meta.
    Mitigation: add company progression, courier collection, weekly cups, streak rewards.
  • Risk: Monetization pressure breaks trust.
    Mitigation: keep skill-based fairness; use cosmetics + optional rewarded boosts only.
