Prototype 01

Photon Rally · Pulse Lane Drift report

Daily 90-second sprints where three neon lanes demand combo timing, buffer trade-offs, and reactor stability.

Back to hero Catalog

Strategy

  • Position as a “micro-burst runner” — 90-second sessions that can be completed between meetings while still feeling layered.
  • Highlight lane balancing decisions over pure tapping to justify the “quality-first” tag and offset hyper-casual comparisons.
  • A/B test gating deeper combos behind streaks so that power players unlock new lanes while casual players still feel rewarded.

SEO / ASO / SEM

  • Primary keywords: "photon runner", "neon puzzle runner", "90-second combos", "daily lane drift".
  • App Store preview: "Triage three glowing lanes, trigger overdrive, and keep the reactor stable in short timed sprints." Add video showing lane toggles + combo multiplier jumps.
  • SEM: spend small budget on appeal terms like "daily energy challenge" with CTA “Tap to stabilize the reactor in under 2 minutes”.
  • Cross-promote via trend article about "meditative focusing games", linking to prototype index and encouraging Discord/Reddit feedback loops.

Loop

  • Start: 90-second timer, lane priorities refresh, tutorial overlay for two combo tiers.
  • Mid: tap to route photons, chain combos to keep energy buffer up, and optionally trigger overdrive with collected reactor samples.
  • End: calculate score (cash + stability), convert combos into progress toward streaks, and unlock a lane story or daily modifier.

Rewards

  • Flavorful Reactor Samples (collect 18 per day) -> cosmetic skins for lane outlines and a daily summary badge.
  • Weekly streak path that unlocks “Momentum Echo” glimpses showing future lane patterns.
  • Shadow runs: replay a high-score session to practice lane weaving and compare combos.

KPIs

  • D1 retention target 42% / D7 21% driven by short-run gating + pushable streak reminders.
  • Session length 90-110s; aim for 2.7 runs per day per active user via quick restart CTA.
  • Combo completion: want ≥75% of players scoring 18+ combos to validate depth vs. fatigue.

Experiments

  • Experiment A: Lane cooldown (1.2× combo) vs. steady drift (flat 1.05×) to see which lifts Royce retention.
  • Experiment B: Visual aura feedback tied to stability (color-coded outline) to test clarity of lane health.
  • Experiment C: “Silent run” mode that hides timers and uses haptics for combos → measure engagement vs. clarity.

Risks

  • Touch latency: lane swaps require precise taps; poor responsiveness will make drift feel unfair.
  • Communication: without a strong story, players may not understand why stability matters, reducing retention.
  • Overdrive clarity: if multipliers feel opaque, players may threaten stability just to chase scores, hurting KPI goals.
